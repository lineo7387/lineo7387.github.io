# Welcome to Bauhaus Blog

The Bauhaus movement revolutionized design in the early 20th century, and its principles remain remarkably relevant today. This blog explores how the fusion of craftsmanship and fine arts created a new visual language that continues to influence modern design.

## The Core Principles

The Bauhaus school, founded in 1919 by Walter Gropius, established three fundamental principles:

1. **Form follows function** — Every element should serve a purpose
2. **Unity of art and technology** — Design should embrace modern manufacturing
3. **Geometric purity** — Clean shapes create visual harmony

## Why Bauhaus Matters Today

In an era of cluttered interfaces and visual noise, Bauhaus principles offer a path to clarity. By embracing:

- Primary colors as accents
- Geometric shapes as design elements
- Typography as a visual tool
- Negative space as a composition element

Designers can create interfaces that are both beautiful and functional.
